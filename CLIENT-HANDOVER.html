01How it all fits together

Four separate services do four different jobs. Almost every question below comes down to knowing which one owns the thing you want to change.

ServiceWhat it doesYou'll use it
Guesty The master record for your properties: prices, availability, calendars, bookings. Everything about money and dates starts here. Daily
Webflow The website itself — pages, photos, descriptions, design. Often
Railway The engine room. A small program that connects the website to Guesty and PayU. It runs by itself. Almost never
PayU Takes the guest's card payment. For refunds and settlements

What happens when a guest books

  1. Guest picks dates on your website.
  2. The website asks Guesty, live, what those exact dates cost. The price shown is always real and current — it is never a stored number.
  3. Guest clicks Book Now and fills in their details.
  4. They pay through PayU. Card details never touch your website or your systems.
  5. Once payment clears, the reservation is created in Guesty automatically and the guest sees a confirmation page.
  6. A record of the booking is also saved in Webflow for your reference.

Nobody has to do anything manually for that to happen. It works overnight, on weekends, and while you're on holiday.

02The emergency switch

If something ever goes badly wrong with pricing or payments, this turns the booking system off in about two minutes without breaking your website.

How to switch off online booking

  1. Open Webflow → Site settings → Custom code → Footer code.
  2. Scroll to the very bottom. You'll find a short block that looks like this:
window.VIRAALAY_BOOKING = { apiBase: "https://viraalay-production.up.railway.app" };
  1. Delete the web address between the quote marks so it reads apiBase: ""
  2. Save, then Publish the site.

Your website stays completely normal — photos, descriptions, enquiry forms, WhatsApp and phone buttons all keep working. Only live pricing and online payment stop. Put the address back and publish again to switch it on.

Take a photo or screenshot of that footer block now, before you ever need it. If you can't remember the exact web address, you can't switch the system back on.

03Golden rules

Never

Never change a price or block dates in Webflow. Guesty is the master record. Anything you type into Webflow will be silently overwritten within six hours, and in the meantime your website and your calendar disagree — which is how a villa gets double-booked.

Never

Never delete or edit the code blocks in Webflow's site settings (Custom code → Head and Footer), except for the one emergency line in section 2. These run your search bar, calendar and checkout. Deleting them breaks the site.

Never

Never delete a property from Webflow. If a villa leaves your portfolio, deactivate it in Guesty instead. Deleting in Webflow leaves broken links and the system will try to recreate it.

Always

Always make pricing, availability and calendar changes in Guesty. The website follows automatically. You never need to touch the website to change a rate.

Always

Always keep a valid payment card on the Railway account. If that card fails, the engine room stops and your site can no longer show prices or take bookings. Nothing will warn you in advance.

Careful

If you rename a property or rewrite its "About this home" text in Webflow, that change is sent to Guesty too. These are the only two fields that travel in that direction. Everything else flows Guesty → website only.

Careful

If a designer changes the property page layout — especially the price box or the Book Now button — have someone test a real booking afterwards. The system finds those elements by their appearance on the page, so a redesign can quietly disconnect them.

06Everyday tasks

Adding a new villa

  1. Create and activate the listing in Guesty as you normally would, with photos, rates and calendar.
  2. Within six hours it appears in Webflow under Properties as an unpublished draft.
  3. Open it, check the description and photos read well, add anything missing.
  4. Publish the item. It's now on the website.

The draft step is deliberate — no villa goes live on your website without a human looking at it first.

Taking a villa off the site

Deactivate or unlist it in Guesty. Don't delete the Webflow item.

Changing a rate for a specific date range

Guesty calendar only. Refresh the property page on the website afterwards and you'll see the new figure straight away.

Editing photos or wording

Webflow → Properties → the villa. Photos, galleries and highlights are yours to manage. If you want to be certain the system never overwrites your wording, switch on Lock editorial content on that property.

Checking recent bookings

Guesty is the real record. Webflow → Bookings also keeps a copy with the guest's contact details and payment reference, which is handy for support queries.

The safety check on every payment

When PayU reports a successful payment, the system independently verifies the amount and the payment's authenticity before creating the reservation. A tampered or mismatched payment is refused and sent to a failure page rather than being confirmed. If that ever happens, the guest is told not to pay again and you'll see the booking flagged.

If a guest pays but doesn't get a confirmation

Their money is safe and the payment is recorded. Check Guesty for the reservation and the Webflow Bookings entry for the payment reference. Contact them with a confirmation manually. This should be rare, but a guest emailing about it is not evidence they were charged twice.

09Alerts you'll receive

An automatic health check runs against the booking system and emails the GitHub account owner if it fails. It checks that the engine room is answering and that live prices are still coming through from Guesty.

If you get a failure email

  1. Open the website and try to price a stay on any property page.
  2. If prices show correctly, it was a brief blip — no action needed.
  3. If prices are missing or you see "We could not price this stay", go to section 10 and contact your developer.
  4. If it will be a long outage, use the emergency switch in section 2 so guests see a clean enquiry-only site instead of errors.
Worth knowing

This check is a free safety net, not a guarantee. It sometimes runs late or not at all. Silence does not prove the site is healthy — it's worth someone opening a property page each morning and confirming a price appears. That ten-second habit is the most reliable monitoring you have.

12Before you launch

These are open items at handover. None stop the system working; all should be closed before you promote the site publicly.

  • Do one real test booking and refund it. Payments are in live mode and have never been tested end to end with a real card. Use the cheapest villa, book one night, confirm it appears in Guesty, then refund it in PayU.
  • Change all the passwords and keys. The Guesty, Webflow, PayU and website credentials were shared over chat during the build. Your developer should reissue all four — the PayU salt first, as it protects payments.
  • Point viraalay.com at the website. It currently goes somewhere else. The booking system is already configured to accept it.
  • Delete the 30 sample reviews. Filter Webflow's Reviews by the "Placeholder" switch. Twenty real Airbnb reviews stay.
  • Replace the placeholder brochure PDF and video link.
  • Fix the FAQs section on property pages — 190 real FAQs exist but none display.
  • Show more than 15 properties on the listings page — one villa is currently hidden by a page limit.
  • Fix the cancellation wording at checkout — it still reads "Cancellation terms for this home will appear here."
  • Display the Tagline field, which is filled in but shown nowhere.
  • Add a free uptime monitor such as UptimeRobot, for alerting you can rely on.

13Known limits

Honest list of what this system does not do, so nothing comes as a surprise later.

  • Rupees only. No other currency has been tested.
  • Refunds are manual in PayU, and the booking must be updated by hand afterwards.
  • Rooms are not separately bookable. A guest books the whole property. Where individual rooms are sold — the BlueRoot Heritage rooms — each is set up in Guesty as its own listing.
  • The "Rooms" selector on the booking box doesn't change the price. Guesty prices the whole property.
  • Guest details sit in the Webflow bookings list with no automatic clean-up. Anyone with Webflow access can read them.
  • Cancellation policy text is not yet displayed at checkout.
  • New properties need a human to publish them. Deliberate, but it does mean a villa added on Friday evening won't be bookable until someone approves it.
  • Code changes need your developer to deploy them. Nothing deploys automatically.

14Questions and answers

If I change a price in Guesty, do I need to do anything on the website?

No. A guest choosing dates gets a price fetched from Guesty at that moment. The "from" price on homepage cards catches up within six hours on its own.

Do I need to press Publish in Webflow after changing something in Guesty?

No. The system publishes those updates itself. You only press Publish for changes you make in Webflow.

Can two guests book the same villa for the same dates?

Availability is checked against Guesty at the moment of booking, and Guesty is also the master calendar for Airbnb and your other channels — so a date booked anywhere stops being offered everywhere. The only theoretical risk is two people paying within the same few seconds. If it ever happens, you'll see it in Guesty and can refund one guest.

What if someone edits a price in Webflow by mistake?

No harm to guests — they're always charged the live Guesty price. The website will correct itself within six hours.

Is guest card data stored anywhere in our systems?

No. Card details are entered on PayU's own pages and never pass through your website, Guesty or the engine room. You do store names, emails and phone numbers in Guesty and in the Webflow bookings list.

What happens if Railway goes down?

The website stays up — photos, descriptions, enquiry forms, phone and WhatsApp all work. Live prices and online booking stop. Guests can still contact you. Use the emergency switch in section 2 if it will be a long outage, so nobody sees an error.

Can we take a deposit instead of the full amount?

The system supports it and it's currently set to charge the full stay. Changing it to a percentage deposit is a small configuration change for your developer, not a rebuild.

Can we add a new destination city?

Yes. Add properties in that city in Guesty and ask your developer to add the city to the search box's list — it's a one-line change. Cities with no Guesty properties are deliberately not offered, so guests never search for something that returns nothing.

What if we redesign the property page?

Test a full booking afterwards. The price box and Book Now button are found by how they appear on the page, so restyling can disconnect them silently — the page will look fine while quietly not working.

Who owns the code?

You do. It sits in a GitHub repository. Note it is currently publicly visible — it contains no passwords, but anyone can read how the system is built. Ask your developer to make it private if you'd rather, though that reduces the free automated checks.

Can we move away from Railway to something free?

You can, but it's a false economy at this scale. The free alternatives either put the site to sleep between visits — which breaks the connection to Guesty and takes pricing down — or forbid commercial use, or need the system rebuilt. Five dollars a month is less than one lost booking.

Does a guest see a price including GST?

Yes. Every figure shown to a guest includes taxes, and the checkout itemises the accommodation fare and GST separately before they pay.

How do we know the system is working right now?

Open any property page, pick dates a few weeks out, and check a price with a tax breakdown appears. That single test exercises the whole chain: website, engine room, and Guesty.

What's the one thing most likely to break it?

The Railway payment card failing, or someone deleting the code blocks in Webflow's site settings. Both are entirely preventable.
